Meet Barley

Arf! Barley needs help finding his forever home

Barley is an incredibly sweet, playful, and goofy little guy with huge heart and a wonderful personality. Born a humble street puppy in Guatemala, he traveled a long way to the Bay Area, where he's discovered that he really needs a quieter lifestyle and environment than he's had so far in busy San Francisco.

Barley would do really well in a home with a yard, away from major traffic, perhaps in a suburban, semi rural, or rural area. He'd really like a family where someone is home most of the time. Bonus points for another dog that could be a furry mentor.

Things Barley loves

  • Fluffy, plushy toys
  • Long walks on the beach (yes, literally)
  • Meeting and greeting other doggos - he loves to make new puppy pals and is great with other dogs!
  • Curling up at your feet for naps and pats
  • Taking a weirdly long time to eat a small carrot
  • Playing with puzzle toys (especially tricky food dispensing ones!)
  • Being with his favorite people
  • So much more! When Barley's in the right environment, his tail is always waggin' away.

Barley's special requests

Barley is, at his core, a *very* good boy who desperately wants to please. However, he has some pretty meaningful anxiety that is particularly exacerbated by his currently loud and unpredictable urban environment. He's manifested his stress with mild separation anxiety that we've had good success working though so far, though he can experience setbacks when he's feeling acute anxiety, most of which is directly related to scary things outside like buses and moving trucks overwhelming him.

He's also wary of new people, and can be barky and growly when new folks enter the house for the first time, though he warms up to them, especially when treats are brought into the equation :)

We strongly feel that with continued training and a less stressful home location, Barley can surpass these issues and be the affectionate, wonderful pup he truly wants to be all the time. His new family will have plenty of support in this process! Training sessions with certified, positive reinforcement trainers, plus remote sessions with Linda from Unidos Para Los Animales (Barley's original rescue organization), will be available to make the transition as positive as possible.
