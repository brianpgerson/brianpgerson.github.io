My Story

My path into software development started with markup. Building HTML and CSS templates for emails and static pages at work led me to build a website for friend's startup idea (for free, in exchange for patience). That first encounter with a real project (and programming in JavaScript) was a game changer.

I started staying late at the office, reading books like Eloquent JavaScript and The Good Parts, and building personal projects. Some of the engineers worked late too - they noticed, and gave me more to do. Introduced me to pair programming and drilled me on OOP principles. Made me feel like I was one of them already. I was rushing through my workday to get to the things I couldn't stop thinking about. I was hooked.

So when that company got bought in 2015, it was time to make my evening work my fulltime job. I joined the most rigorous Bay Area bootcamp I could find and devoted 1000+ hours over the course of three months to putting polish on my years of self-teaching, coding full-time and building out a portfolio in the process.

These days, you can find me building projects primarily in Ruby and JavaScript with tools like Rails and React. Feel free to check out a few recent ones below, and get in touch if you're looking for a web developer with an insatiable appetite for learning, a knack for (and startup experience in) product design, and an uncanny ability to befriend new dogs.
